1.

Work experience or traineeship

You could take the work experience route to this job. You could do an unpaid Traineeship for up to 6 months and gain unpaid work experience which could lead to paid work experience. Or, if you stay in school and get your GCSEs, you could gain paid work experience.

You could do unpaid work as an Administration Assistant in a legal firm, or department, and work your way up to Legal Secretary. From there, you could work your way up to Paralegal. This would take time, but you would earn a salary.

2.

Vocational route

You could take the vocational route to this job. You would need to stay in school to gain your GCSEs A-C, which would allow you to take an Advanced Apprenticeship. An Advanced Apprenticeship takes 2 years and you would earn money.

With this qualification, you could enter the profession as a Legal Secretary and work your way up to Paralegal while earning money. Or, you could take a Higher Apprenticeship, which takes 2 years and you would earn money. With this qualification, you could go on to do a Degree Apprenticeship as a Paralegal, which takes three years and you would earn a salary.

More information

3.

Academic route

You could take the academic route to this job. Once you have your GCSEs, you could stay in school and do A levels.

With A levels you could do a degree, which takes 3 years and you will pay fees. This would allow you to enter the profession at a higher level and earn a higher salary. With a degree, you could go on to study at postgraduate level and become a QC, work at the Bar or become a Solicitor.

Also, with A levels, you could follow the Apprenticeship route by taking a Higher Apprenticeship which takes two years and you earn a salary. Then you could take a Degree Apprenticeship as a Paralegal.

Advanced Apprenticeship

You could take the vocational route to this job with GCSEs. If you have 4 GCSEs A* to C, you could take an Advanced Apprenticeship. An Advanced Apprenticeship takes 2 years and you would earn money.

With this qualification, you could enter the profession as a Legal Secretary and work your way up to Paralegal while earning money. Or, you could take a Higher Apprenticeship, which takes 2 more years and you would earn money.

With a Higher Apprenticeship you could do a Degree Apprenticeship as a Paralegal, which takes three years and you would earn a salary.

1.

Volunteering, traineeships & apprenticeships

To be able to start your nursing career you could try volunteering at a hospital to gain experience.

You could do a Traineeship for up to 6 months and gain unpaid work experience which could lead to an apprenticeship.

If you choose this route you will be paid as you train, but you will still have to do a degree to become a qualified nurse.

You may also be able to take part in a cadet scheme. This can help you decide on a career route to follow and may lead to work in a number of different areas, including nursing or supporting roles with or without patient contact.
