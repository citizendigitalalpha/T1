Redundancy

Redundancy isn't usually welcome, but it could be a good opportunity to change job or career.

If you’re made redundant, try to keep calm and stay positive. It will help if you:

  • see redundancy as an opportunity for change
  • focus on moving forward rather than looking back
  • look at your options
  • get advice from professional advisers
  • talk to your friends and family
  • find out your transferable skills

Don't take redundancy personally. Remember that it’s the job that has been made redundant, not you. A lot of people face redundancy at some point in their lives.

Try not to feel rushed into making decisions. Although you might have concerns about money, a quick fix might not be the best thing in the long term. Weigh up all your options carefully – this way you'll make the best decision.

Check your rights if you’ve been made redundant.

Before you leave your employer, make sure you:

  • pick up your P45
  • get written details of your redundancy payment and package

Get the contact details of your:

  • line manager
  • trade union representative
  • human resources department
  • pension fund trustees

Your employer might provide free careers guidance to help you decide on your next move. Some will offer money for training.

You can get advice on redundancy and financial issues from:

  • your trade union
  • professional bodies and associations
  • your local Citizens Advice
  • independent financial advisers
  • employment law experts

Redundancy can be a stressful time but there are people who can help you cope. You can speak to a counsellor, who can support you in moving on. Your employer may help you arrange this. The British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy may also be able to help.
