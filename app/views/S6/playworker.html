Playworker Also known as: After school worker, Nursery worker, Childcare

Playworkers plan, organise and take part in play and leisure activities for children between 4 and 16.

1. Entry requirements

You don’t need any formal qualifications, but it will help if you have some experience, either paid or as a volunteer. You could prepare for this job by taking a college course in playwork.

You'll need:

  • to get on well with children, parents and carers
  • patience and tolerance
  • a responsible and caring attitude
  • to be active and fairly fit

Before you start paid or voluntary work, you'll need to pass background checks from the Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS).

You could get into this job through an apprenticeship.

Information Apprenticeships are available to anyone over the age of 16

Employers may be entitiled to get funding to support this. Or anyone over 24 may be able to apply for an advanced learner loan, to help fund an apprenticeship.

2. Skills required

You'll need:

  • good listening skills
  • creativity, to plan interesting play sessions
  • the ability to work well as part of a team
  • good organisational skills

3. What you'll do

Your day-to-day duties may include:

  • arranging art, crafts, cooking and drama activities
  • playing outdoor games with children
  • taking children on outings
  • encouraging children to be independent
  • planning activities with the children
  • providing play areas, materials and equipment
  • talking to children about their concerns or worries
  • dealing with injuries and emergencies
  • liaising with parents, carers, and sometimes other professionals
  • keeping records and looking after petty cash

4. Salary

  • Starter:
    £12,000 or more
  • Experienced:
    £20,000
  • Highly experienced:
    £25,000 to £30,000*

    * Manager

If you work part-time, you may be paid pro-rata (a percentage of the full-time salary) or on an hourly basis.

These figures are a guide.

5. Working hours, patterns and environment

Most playwork is in the evenings, at weekends and during school holidays.

You'll probably be working indoors and outdoors, depending on location and the type of activity. Your work will usually be very active, and can involve joining in with games and sports.

6. Career path and progression

With experience and training, you could become a manager or work for a local authority as a playwork development officer.
